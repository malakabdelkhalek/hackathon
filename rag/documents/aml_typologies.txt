AML TYPOLOGIES — FATF REFERENCE DOCUMENT

TYPOLOGY 1: SMURFING (STRUCTURING)
Definition: Breaking down large amounts into smaller transactions to avoid reporting thresholds.
Indicators: Multiple transactions just below 10,000 EUR threshold, same destination account, short time intervals between transactions.
Reference: FATF Recommendation 16, AML 6th Directive Article 3.

TYPOLOGY 2: LAYERING VIA OFFSHORE ACCOUNTS
Definition: Moving funds through multiple jurisdictions to obscure the origin of funds.
Indicators: Transfers to Malta, Cyprus, UAE, British Virgin Islands. Vague memos such as consulting or invoice with no supporting documentation.
Reference: FATF Recommendation 13.

TYPOLOGY 3: POLITICALLY EXPOSED PERSONS (PEP) TRANSACTIONS
Definition: Transactions involving PEPs carry elevated risk of corruption and bribery.
Indicators: PEP status confirmed, large transfers, offshore destinations, no clear business purpose documented.
Reference: AML 6th Directive Article 20.

TYPOLOGY 4: RAPID MOVEMENT OF FUNDS
Definition: Funds transferred in and out of an account within 48 hours.
Indicators: Large credit followed immediately by large debit, no business explanation provided.
Reference: FATF Recommendation 20.

TYPOLOGY 5: INCONSISTENT TRANSACTION PROFILE
Definition: Transaction volume or type inconsistent with client declared source of funds.
Indicators: Retail client with salary income making large corporate-sized transfers repeatedly.
Reference: FATF Recommendation 10, NORDA Internal Policy KYC-07.
