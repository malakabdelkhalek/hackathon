EU AI ACT — RELEVANT PROVISIONS FOR BANKING AI SYSTEMS

ARTICLE 6 — HIGH RISK CLASSIFICATION:
AI systems used in credit scoring, fraud detection, and AML monitoring are classified as HIGH RISK under Annex III.
High-risk AI systems must meet strict requirements before deployment.

ARTICLE 9 — RISK MANAGEMENT:
High-risk AI systems must implement a continuous risk management system.
Risks must be identified, analyzed, and mitigated on an ongoing basis.
Risk management documentation must be maintained and updated.

ARTICLE 13 — TRANSPARENCY AND EXPLAINABILITY:
High-risk AI systems must be transparent in their operation.
Users must be informed when interacting with an AI system.
All decisions must be explainable in plain language to affected persons.

ARTICLE 14 — HUMAN OVERSIGHT:
High-risk AI systems must allow meaningful human oversight.
Humans must be able to intervene, override, and suspend AI decisions.
This requirement is mandatory for all AI systems affecting individual rights or financial decisions.

ARTICLE 17 — QUALITY MANAGEMENT AND AUDIT:
Documentation of AI system purpose, logic, and training data required.
Every decision must be logged and traceable to the specific AI version.
Audit trails must be maintained for minimum 10 years in financial services.
