EU AML 6TH DIRECTIVE — KEY PROVISIONS SUMMARY

ARTICLE 3 — CRIMINAL OFFENSES:
Money laundering is a criminal offense across all EU member states.
Banks must report suspicious activity to Financial Intelligence Units.
Mandatory reporting threshold: transactions above 10,000 EUR in cash.

ARTICLE 6 — LIABILITY:
Legal persons including companies can be held liable for AML failures.
Penalties include fines up to 10 percent of annual turnover.

ARTICLE 7 — SANCTIONS:
Member states must impose effective sanctions for non-compliance.
Sanctions must be dissuasive and proportionate to the violation.

ARTICLE 20 — PEP ENHANCED DUE DILIGENCE:
Politically Exposed Persons require enhanced due diligence procedures.
Banks must identify source of wealth and source of funds for all PEPs.
Senior management approval required before establishing PEP relationships.

ARTICLE 23 — SUSPICIOUS TRANSACTION REPORTING:
Banks must file Suspicious Activity Reports within 24 hours of detection.
Filing a SAR does not constitute breach of client confidentiality.
Tipping off a suspected client about a SAR is a criminal offense.
